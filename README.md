# Silent and Invisible 



## Air Quality, Slow Violence, and the Limits of Measurement



## Overview

This project explores air pollution as a form of **slow violence**—a distributed, invisible, and cumulative harm that is difficult to perceive, represent, and act upon.

While air pollution is widely recognised as a major global health risk, it is often reduced to simplified metrics such as the **Air Quality Index (AQI)**. This project critically examines how such systems of measurement may obscure the true scale, complexity, and lived experience of environmental harm.

---

## Research Focus

The project investigates three key themes:

### 1. Air Pollution as Slow Violence
Drawing on Rob Nixon’s concept of *slow violence*, this project frames air pollution as:
- Gradual and accumulative
- Statistically represented rather than directly visible
- Unevenly distributed across populations

It challenges the idea that pollution is “out of sight”, instead considering how it is differently experienced and perceived.

---

### 2. Limits of the Air Quality Index (AQI)

The AQI translates multiple pollutants (PM2.5, NO₂, O₃, etc.) into a **single scalar value**, typically determined by the highest pollutant level.

This project critiques AQI as:
- A **reductive model** of environmental conditions  
- A system that prioritises **readability over accuracy**  
- A metric that obscures **cumulative and multi-pollutant exposure**

---

### 3. Health Impacts of Air Pollution

Air pollution is linked to a wide range of diseases, including:
- Lung cancer  
- Cardiovascular disease  
- Asthma and bronchitis  
- Type 2 diabetes  

These impacts are:
- Multi-systemic  
- Long-term  
- Often underrepresented in public-facing data systems  

---

## Key Questions

- How does air pollution become invisible despite its widespread impact?
- What is lost when complex environmental data is reduced to a single index?
- How do measurement systems shape public understanding and policy priorities?
- Can alternative forms of representation make slow violence more perceptible?

---

## Methodology

This project combines:
- **Literature review** (environmental theory, public health, policy)
- **Critical analysis** of AQI systems and data visualisation
- **Design experimentation** exploring alternative ways of representing air quality

## Exhibition Guide

This work is designed to be interacted with. When setting up, please ensure it's placed in a well lit room with lots of natural light, with a balance for users to see the screen with no glare, but not enough brightness on screen for the reflection to impact the optical flow library. 

Setup in a space where the user has a lot of room to move around; preferably 3m wide and 2m tall, with a large-scale screen to match.
